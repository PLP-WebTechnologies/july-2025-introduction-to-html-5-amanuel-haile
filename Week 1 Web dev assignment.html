How to Avoid the Headache of Blood‑Type Matching & Issuance in Emergencies

Home About Contact Us

In emergency situations, when patient blood type is unknown, it is standard practice to issue O negative red cells. This prevents ABO or RhD incompatibility reactions.

The following job aid outlines:

  • Why blood typing matters
  • How to handle unknown-donor situations
  • Protocols for issuing red cells, plasma, and platelets

When issuing plasma, AB positive plasma can safely be given to recipients of any blood type. Platelets, being largely cell‑free, are typically issued without cross‑matching.
